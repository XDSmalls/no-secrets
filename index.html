No more secrets; my family's Italian recipes are here for the taking.

Buon Appetito

You know that dish...the one that never tastes quite right when you try to make it yourself?

There's a reason for that (it's not just the recipe you were given)...

For generations, certain recipes moved through families with the level of protection given to business' intellectual property. Like a game of telephone, they changed in the telling, something often left out. It comes less from a place of malice so much as love, or just the unspoken assumption that some things should stay where and as they are.

No Secrets is a collection of my family's recipes, the real ones, down to the ratios, temperatures, techniques, etc. + the epic Italian kitchen lore of where they come from and why they matter, with the best restaurant recommendations for the cities that have made me a gastrophile, and some personal exploration on the side. Please enjoy!

— Samuele

Why I made this

If you ask me where I'm from, I'll tell you Italian, South African, or American, two of the three, or all of them, depending on the context. At some point I decided that it didn't really matter all that much where I'm from. I am a definite mix of all three, and in my heart, I don't know "where I'm from," but I was definitely raised in the kitchen.

I started this site to answer some common FAQs:

Q: "Can you give me your recipe for that?"

A: "Sure." then never remember to

Q: "Why doesn't it come out like when you made it for me?"

A: I probably don't even know what I did differently

Q: "Please send me all of your food recommendations for Italy"

A: I do, but it's never as complete as it could be.

Q: "You're being pretentious; if you like food so much why don't you become a chef or write about it?"

A: "Boh"

My hope is that through this site I am able to answer all of these questions more completely through creating a one-stop shop for my passion for gastronomy, share it with others, and support a more open & shared ecosystem of classical recipes, and hopefully cultivate a passion for food in others.

For more about why I care about any of this stuff, visit this page.
