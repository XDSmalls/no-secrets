Pasta Ricotta Salata

Pasta salad

A classic Italian summer recipe I fell in love with before I can even remember. Somehow it's perfect warm or cold: refreshing, light, and filling all at once. Given how simple it is, there isn't much lore here, just good times spent in the warm summer sun, gulping down spoonfuls of this soul-healing concoction.

Cook time 30 min
Serves 6
Difficulty 2 / 5
Cost ★★★★★
Popularity ★★★★★

Ingredients

  • Dry ricotta salata (one block)
  • Cherry tomatoes, heirloom if you can find them
  • Arugula
  • Fresh basil
  • Pasta (a single shape)
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper

Method

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil for the pasta. The moment it hits a rolling boil, salt it generously and add your pasta. Stick to a single shape here, as it keeps the texture of the finished dish consistent. While the pasta cooks, prep the salad components below.
  2. The cheese. Grate the whole block of dry ricotta salata into a bowl and sprinkle in about 2 teaspoons of black pepper. Finely chop the arugula, fine but not too fine, or its flavor disappears into the dish's other strong flavors. Add the arugula to the cheese and fold them together no more than six times; any more and the cheese starts to clump, which throws off the texture.
  3. The tomatoes. In a separate bowl, quarter the cherry tomatoes. Add 2 teaspoons each of salt and pepper and about a cup of oil, scaling those amounts up for every two boxes of tomatoes you use. Mix once, just to distribute the salt, pepper, and oil, then let it sit for 10 minutes. Don't combine it with the cheese until the pasta is cooked.
  4. Bring it together. Once the pasta is done, tip the tomato mixture into the bowl of cheese and arugula and stir to combine. Drain the pasta, add it to a large empty bowl, and pour the cheese-and-tomato mixture over the top. Toss with a utensil of your choice until the cheese and tomatoes are evenly distributed throughout.
  5. Finish. Finely chop six large basil leaves into very small pieces and scatter them over the bowl. Toss three more times, and your pasta salad is ready to enjoy.
